Private Client & Family Law FAQs

Ghana recognizes several grounds for divorce including adultery, unreasonable behavior, desertion for at least two years, and separation for at least two years with consent or five years without consent. Our family law attorneys can guide you through the appropriate grounds based on your specific circumstances and help you understand the legal implications and requirements for filing divorce proceedings.

Child custody decisions prioritize the best interests of the child, considering factors including the child's welfare, emotional needs, and relationship with each parent. Ghanaian courts examine parental capability, living arrangements, and the child's preferences when appropriate. Our attorneys advocate for custody arrangements that protect your children's wellbeing while preserving your parental rights.

Estate planning encompasses will preparation, trust formation, asset protection strategies, and succession planning to ensure your wealth is preserved and transferred according to your wishes. Our private client services help minimize tax implications, avoid family disputes, and provide for your loved ones' future security through comprehensive legal planning tailored to your family's unique needs.
